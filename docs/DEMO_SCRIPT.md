# Five-minute demo script

## 0:00–0:30 — Thesis

Open the replay at kickoff.

> Every fan witnesses a slightly different live match. Match DNA turns those imperfect witness views into one canonical memory, then seals that memory against TxLINE commitments on Solana.

Point out the three materials: liquid, amber, crystal.

## 0:30–1:20 — Witness divergence

Play at 12× and pause around the first delayed/reordered event. Show the reconciliation toast.

> The browser is not canonical. Source timestamps and source identities drive the journal; arrival time only drives what this witness saw.

Let the liquid ring settle into amber.

## 1:20–2:10 — Delayed proof

Select a deliberately delayed ring.

> The interval is closed and its geometry is locked, but the root is not available yet. Amber is a first-class state, so a slow proof does not break the experience.

Advance until the ring crystallizes without changing shape.

## 2:10–3:00 — Emotional grammar

Jump to the red card, comeback goal, and late equalizer scare. Show uncertainty, fragility, draw threat, volatility, and bounded shock.

> Entropy alone misses a fragile late 1–0. The separate fragility channel increases as the same failure probability is compressed into fewer remaining minutes.

## 3:00–3:45 — Proof microscope

Open the proof tab. Show:

- canonical ring hash;
- closing odds and score leaves;
- Merkle siblings;
- proof bundle hash;
- root PDA reference;
- material transition.

Clearly state that the bundled replay uses synthetic vectors and that the same adapter accepts real TxLINE payloads.

## 3:45–4:25 — Commit/reveal

Open Witness. Change allegiance or lens and show the commitment change. Commit before kickoff and reveal after final proof.

> The final phenotype mixes a precommitted secret with verified match leaves, so a fan cannot choose a seed after seeing the result.

## 4:25–5:00 — Durable output and SDK

Download the canonical SVG/WAV/timeline, then show the monorepo packages and the CLI verifier.

> This is not only a visual demo. It is a reusable canonicalization and proof-finality SDK that other hackathon teams can import.
